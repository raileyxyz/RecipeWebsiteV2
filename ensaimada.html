Ensaimada

Soft, buttery ensaymada that melts in your mouth. Perfectly fluffy, lightly sweet, and topped with creamy butter, sugar, and grated cheese. A comforting treat for breakfast, merienda, or any time you crave a taste of home.

Procedures

  1. Combine warm milk, yeast, and sugar, and stir until yeast and sugar dissolve in milk. Set aside to activate (about 10 minutes).
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine all the dry ingredients: flour, sugar, and salt. Mix thoroughly.
  3. Add eggs, butter, and the activated yeast mixture. Mix until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough until smooth and fine in texture.
  5. Place the dough in a bowl, cover with a cloth, and let it rise for 2 hours.
  6. Flatten the dough on a surface and punch lightly to release air.
  7. Divide the dough into four pieces using a dough slicer.
  8. Roll each piece into a cylindrical shape and cut into individual servings (around 80 g each).
  9. Flatten each piece with a rolling pin.
  10. Mix melted butter and white sugar. Brush the mixture on the center of each flattened dough.
  11. Sprinkle cheese on top, then seal and roll the dough into a long cylinder (about 14 inches).
  12. Form the spiral Ensaimada shape by crossing the ends and tucking them in the center.
  13. Place on a tray with wax paper, cover, and let rise for another 45 minutes.
