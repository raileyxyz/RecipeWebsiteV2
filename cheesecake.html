Cheesecake

Cheesecake is a dessert made with a creamy filling of cheese, eggs, and sugar over a crust, typically made from crushed cookies or graham crackers. It is known for its rich, smooth, and dense texture and can be either baked or unbaked, served chilled. Variations include different types of cheese (like cream cheese for New York-style), unique crusts, and various toppings, such as fruit sauces, whipped cream, or chocolate.

Procedures

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Lightly spray a 9″ springform pan with non-stick spray.
  3. Wrap the bottom and sides of the pan with heavy-duty tinfoil (use several layers to prevent water from seeping in during the water bath).
  4. Set the pan aside.
  5. In a large bowl, combine graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and melted butter; mix well.
  6. Firmly press the mixture into the prepared pan.
  7. Bake for 8 minutes.
  8. Remove and place on a cooling rack while preparing the filling.
  9. In a high-power blender, food processor, stand mixer (with whisk attachment), or large bowl with a hand mixer, beat softened cream cheese until completely smooth, scraping the bowl as needed.
  10. Add sugar and vanilla; beat until smooth, scraping down the sides and bottom of the bowl.
  11. Add eggs and yolks one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  12. Add the cream and mix until just incorporated.
  13. Pour the filling into the prepared crust and smooth the top with a silicone spatula.
  14. Place the cheesecake pan into a large, deep pan and fill with 2 inches of hot water (this creates the water bath).
  15. Carefully place in the oven and bake for 1 hour and 10 minutes.
  16. Turn the oven off and let the cheesecake sit inside (door closed) for 45 minutes — it should still be slightly wiggly in the center.
  17. Remove from oven and gently run a knife around the edge of the cake.
  18. Place on a cooling rack and let cool completely.
  19. Loosely cover with plastic wrap and chill for at least 8 hours.
  20. Cheesecake will keep covered in the fridge for up to 5 days.
